Our team of graduate students from the Medill National Security Reporting Project, in collaboration with VICE News, spent three months reporting on whether public health officials around the world are doing enough to prepare for the next outbreak of a deadly infectious disease that could kill thousands, or even millions, of people.

We traveled to teeming refugee communities in Karachi, Pakistan to chronicle the alarming resurgence of polio, and to the bat caves of Tao Pun, Thailand, where officials fear the next Ebola-type virus may be incubating. We traveled to Atlanta to report on the premiere U.S. global disease detection program at the U.S. Centers for Disease Control, and interviewed more than 80 experts and health officials on several continents.

This series is being published with the generous assistance of Robert R. McCormick Foundation grants.
